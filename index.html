How Does Property Rental Work?

Renting a property is essentially entering into a long-term agreement to use and occupy that space. Unlike a traditional property purchase, you don't own the property. Instead, a leasing company or property owner allows you to use the space, and you make regular payments for the agreed-upon duration. Some rental agreements, however, may offer the option to purchase the property at the end of the rental period.

Similar to a short-term property lease, there are guidelines that outline:

  1. The extent of property use, specified as annual occupancy (additional usage may incur predetermined charges).
  2. Categories of potential property-related damages for which you may bear responsibility upon the lease conclusion.
  3. The processes and associated costs involved when returning the property at the end of your lease term.

Property lease agreements require a commitment, and terminating them prematurely can be a complex and costly undertaking. Therefore, it is crucial to thoroughly assess your circumstances before making a decision.
